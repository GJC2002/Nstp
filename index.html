Objectives of the Project

  • To improve the nurtional status of underweight children living in Barangay Sitio Halang by providing nutrious meal during the activity
  • Provide balanced meal following the Pinggang Pinoy model
  • Serve at least 100 pre-identified malnourished children in Barangay Sitio Halang
  • Addresses immediate hunger by introducing the concept of a balanced diet.
  • The program should be completed within 4 hours session from 8 am - 12 pm.
  • The goal of the project is to provide 100 children with one nutrient-dense meal and snacks that meets 30% of their daily caloric needs during the 4-hour program.

Project Description:



Background of the Project

Malnutrition remains a challenges in the Philippines, with 26.7% of children under the age of 5 being stunted as of 2024. This "hidden hunger" means 1 in 3 children are small for their age due to chronic lack of nutrients, which can lead to permanent delays in physical and cognitive development. The primary driver of this issue is poverty and food insecurity, affecting roughly 31.4% of Filipino households. Many families cannot afford diverse, protein-rich diets, relying instead on high-carbohydrate fillers like rice. This is often compounded by a lack of access to clean water and health education among parents. Hunger is a major barrier to learning. Malnourished children often suffer from poor concentration, high absenteeism, and lower academic performance. By providing a nutrient-dense meal, even for one day, we can briefly alleviate this burden and demonstrate the importance of a balanced diet (Pinggang Pinoy). This project aligns with Republic Act No. 11037 (the Masustansyang Pagkain para sa Batang Pilipino Act), which mandates a national feeding program to combat undernutrition. As NSTP students, we aim to support this mandate at the grassroots level in Barangay Sitio Halang.

In Barangay Sitio Halang, many families belong to low-income sector where children are often seen eating low nutrient-foods. This project serves as an immediate intervention to provide nutrition and educate families on affordable, healthy alternatives.


Justification of the Project

While our day one program program cannot solve chronic malnutrition, it provides a crucial nutritional boost for children who may not receive a balanced meal daily. By following the Pinggang Pinoy (Healthy Food Plate) model, the project ensures that participants receive high-quality protein, vitamins, and minerals that are often missing from their regular diets. The project goes beyond "giving food" by incorporating hygiene and health education. Teaching children proper handwashing and toothbrushing prevents common infections (like diarrhea or respiratory illnesses) that often cause nutrients to be lost, even when food is available. This creates a lasting habit that survives long after the food is consumed. A major barrier to nutrition in low-income households is the misconception that "healthy" equals "expensive." This project justifies its existence by providing parents with budget-friendly, nutrient-dense recipes. By showing them how to use local, accessible ingredients (like malunggay, squash, or mung beans), we empower the community to continue these practices on their own. As NSTP students, our immersion fosters a sense of volunteerism and social responsibility. This project bridges the gap between the university and the community, creating a supportive environment where local leaders (Barangay health workers) and students work together to monitor and support the well-being of the most vulnerable sector: the children.

Early intervention is key. By raising awareness of proper nutrition today, we help mitigate the risks of stunting and cognitive delays that could affect these children's future school performance and adult productivity.


Benefits Derived from the Project

  • Immediate Nutritional Intake: Access to a high-quality, balanced meal (Pinggang Pinoy style) that meets a significant portion of their daily protein and vitamin requirements.
  • Improved Hygiene Habits: Acquisition of life-saving skills, such as proper 7-step handwashing and toothbrushing, which reduce the risk of diarrhea and parasitic infections.
  • Increased Health Awareness: Early exposure to the importance of vegetables and "Go, Glow, and Grow" foods, helping them make better snack choices in the future.
  • Cost-Effective Nutrition Education: Learning how to prepare nutrient-dense meals (e.g., using malunggay, squash, or mung beans) that fit a tight household budget.
  • Access to Resources: Receipt of hygiene kits and educational flyers that can be used by the entire household.
  • Community Support: A sense of relief and empowerment knowing that there are local initiatives and students concerned with their children's welfare.
  • Health Data Support: Assistance for Barangay Health Workers (BHWs) in identifying or monitoring the nutritional status of children in the area.
  • Strengthened Partnership: Improved relationship and solidarity between the university and the local government unit (LGU).
  • Promotion of Volunteerism: Serving as a model for other local youth groups to start similar small-scale community service projects.
  • Social Awareness: Gaining a first-hand understanding of the "hunger gap" and the socio-economic realities faced by marginalized Filipino families.
  • Leadership and Teamwork: Developing skills in project management, budgeting, and ground-level coordination under pressure.
  • Values Integration: Practical application of the NSTP-CWTS pillars—service, patriotism, and social responsibility—beyond the classroom.


Project Coverage

  1. Geographic Coverage:
    • The Project will be limited to Zone 3 and 4 of Barangay Sitio Halang specifically held at the Zone 3 Basketball Court.
    • Limiting the area ensures that the student volunteers can manage the crowd and that the food reaches the intended local community.

  2. Target Beneficiaries:
    • The program will serve a total of 100 children from age 5 - 12 years old.
    • Priority will be given to children identified as "underweight" or "severely underweight" by the Barangay Health Workers (BHWs)
    • 100 parents/guardians are also covered as participants in the health education and recipe-sharing session.

  3. Temporal Coverage:
    • This One Day program is scheduled on May 7, 2026.
    • The program will run from 8 am to 12 pm.

  4. Subject Matter Coverage
    • Nutritional Intervention: Provision of one (1) full meal consisting of rice, a protein source (e.g., Ginataang Manok with Malunggay), and a fruit/vegetable side.
    • Health Literacy: A lecture-demo on the 7-step handwashing technique and proper toothbrushing.
    • Resource Distribution: Distribution of hygiene kits (soap and toothbrush) and nutritional flyers for parents.

Methodology

  1. Pre-Implementation
    • Site Coordination: Meet with the Barangay Captain and Barangay Health Workers (BHWs) to secure the venue
    • Procurement: Purchase fresh ingredients, hygiene kit supplies (soap, toothbrushes, shampoo), and printing materials for flyers 2-3 days before the event.
    • Meal Prep: Conduct initial food preparation (washing and cutting vegetables) the night before to ensure freshness and speed on the event day.
    • Task Allocation: Assign group roles: Cooking Team, Registration/Documentation Team, and Program/Speaker Team.

  1. Implementation

8:00 am Preparation & Registration Venue Preparation and Signing in Children/Parents
9:00 am Opening Program Short prayer, national anthem, and icebreaker games to build rapport.
9:30 am Educational Demo Live demonstration of 7-step handwashing and toothbrushing. Children practice while volunteers assist
10:00 am Parent’s Talk While children play, a 30-minute talk for parents on affordable healthy recipes and flyer distribution.
10:30 am Hygiene Kit Giving Distribution of hygiene kits as a "graduation gift" for finishing the program.
11:00 am Feeding Program Systematic distribution of the Pinggang Pinoy meal. Volunteers monitor for safety and portioning.
12:00 am Closing Remarks and Cleaning Time Group photo, closing remarks, and ensuring the venue is left spotless.


  1. Post-Implementation
    • Feedback Gathering: Conduct a quick verbal survey or "smiley face" poll with children and parents to see if they enjoyed the meal and learned the handwashing steps.
    • Financial Liquidation: Finalize all receipts and expenses to ensure the budget was followed.
    • Narrative Report: Draft the final NSTP documentation, including Before, During, and After photos, attendance sheets, and reflections from the student group.
